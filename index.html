Introduction to Power Systems

By Power Sparks | Electrical Concepts

A power system is a network used to generate, transmit, and distribute electrical energy to consumers safely and efficiently.

Main Parts of Power System

  • Generation System
  • Transmission System
  • Distribution System

1. Generation

Electricity is generated in power plants using thermal, hydro, solar, wind, or nuclear energy. The generated voltage is usually around 11kV to 25kV.

2. Transmission

The generated voltage is stepped up using transformers to very high voltage (132kV, 220kV, 400kV) to reduce power losses during long-distance transmission.

3. Distribution

Voltage is stepped down near cities and supplied to homes and industries at 230V (single phase) or 415V (three phase).

⚡ Tip: Higher voltage means lower current and less power loss.

Why Protection is Important?

Protection devices like relays and circuit breakers protect equipment during short circuits and overload conditions.

Conclusion

Power systems are the backbone of modern civilization, delivering reliable electricity from power plants to end users.
